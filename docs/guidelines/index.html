Author Guidelines

Official protocols for manuscript submission and universe contributions.

Last Updated: June 2026

Any manuscript failing to meet the foundational criteria outlined below will be rejected prior to formal peer evaluation.

I. General Submission Protocols

  • All submitted texts, data structures, diagrams, schematics, and audio/visual assets must be entirely original. The inclusion of copyrighted material from external entities or real-world intellectual properties is strictly prohibited.
  • Manuscripts must prioritize mathematical or physical formalization. "Brute facts" or anomalous phenomena without underlying systemic logic are not permitted, unless previously established by the Principal Author. Proposals for new foundational brute facts must be submitted to the Editorial Committee for verification of creative and narrative relevance.
  • If an article introduces elements traditionally classified as "anomalous" or "magic", the author must attempt a formalization.
  • The resource M.I.R.A.I. may be referenced exclusively to declare objective truths regarding the local universe described within the specific article, provided it does not contradict previously indexed realities. Any attempt to use M.I.R.A.I. to dictate global, overarching truths outside said local universe requires mandatory consultation and written authorization from the Editorial Committee.
  • The writing of articles with the objective of insulting, ridiculing, or promoting animosity toward specific social demographics (including ethnicity, orientation, religion, or political affiliation) is strictly prohibited. Critique of academic ideas or philosophical frameworks, is permitted, provided it maintains a cold, clinical, and non-offensive tone.

II. Requirements for Personnel & Character Profiles

When introducing new researchers, field agents, or subjects of study within the narrative continuity, authors must adhere to the following psychometric and biographical constraints:

  • Given the strictly institutional nature of the Journal, any personal, political, religious, or identity-based traits of the personnel or subjects that do not directly alter empirical results, experimental data, or protocols shall be omitted due to irrelevance.
  • Character names are arbitrary but must remain within the boundaries of professional realism. Highly eccentric, comical, or overtly offensive names will be flagged or rejected.
  • The inclusion of "Chosen Ones," "Gary Stues," "Mary Sues," or stylized "rebel agents" is strictly forbidden. Furthermore, characters must not exhibit extreme, one-dimensional philosophies (such as unyielding narrative optimism or absolute, edgeless nihilism). Characters should be depicted as flawed, bound by institutional hierarchy, and driven by realistic professional motivations (e.g., academic ambition or curiosity).
  • Personnel must not possess unexplained anomalies, convenient "gadgets" for every scenario, or unearned hyper-competence. All technical skills, intellectual specialized areas, and tactical equipment must be fully justified within the methodology and historical continuity of the article.
  • Authors are prohibited from rewriting or ridiculing previously established characters from the database to elevate their own creations.

III. Requirements for Institutional Affiliations & Factions

The introduction of new departments, corporate subsidiaries, or external organizations must comply with the following structural guidelines:

  • The proposed faction must possess a clear, well-defined operational interest (whether public or classified) that directly contributes to the overarching continuity and expansion of the Theoretical Space.
  • The faction's history, assets, and operational scope must not contradict the established chronological milestones of the Journal.
  • The core objective, scientific focus, or anomalous custody of the new affiliation must be distinct from existing organizations. The Journal will not accept redundant factions.
  • Faction names must be entirely fictional and free of real-world corporate trademarks. The Review Board reserves the right to provide naming feedback and mandate stylistic adjustments before granting final publication clearance.
